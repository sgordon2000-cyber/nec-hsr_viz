What makes it faster?

The Marron Institute proposal calls for bypasses of slow urban segments, electrification of commuter lines, and 220 mph capable trainsets. The key bottleneck today is not top speed — it's slow zones near cities like New Haven and Baltimore where freight traffic forces the Acela to crawl at 30–45 mph.

💰

How much does it cost?

The proposal totals $12.5B in infrastructure plus $4.5B for new trainsets — a fraction of Amtrak's own $117B 15-year plan. By using European technical standards and separating maintenance from megaprojects, costs are compressed dramatically.

🗓️

Frequency improvements

Today's Acela runs roughly every 1–2 hours. The HSR plan enables departures every 10 minutes near NYC and every 15 minutes near Boston and DC — turning rail into something closer to a metro, not an airline.

🌍

Compared to Europe

London to Paris (281 mi) takes 2h 16m on Eurostar. New York to DC (225 mi) currently takes 2h 56m on the Acela. With HSR, NY–DC would be under 2 hours — finally competitive with European intercity rail.
